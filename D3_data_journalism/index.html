Correlations Discovered Between Poverty and Age, By American state

Using data collected from https://factfinder.census.gov for 2014 ACS 1 year estimates, we have visualised the relationships between age and the percentage of people showing as living in poverty.

As we can see in the visualisation above, West Virginia has the median age of 41.9 showing a high poverty rate of 18.3%, Therefore we can conclude they have the highest rate of poverty for the oldest age bracket recorded. Mississippi on the other hand is showing highest poverty rate of 21.5% with the median age of 36.7.
